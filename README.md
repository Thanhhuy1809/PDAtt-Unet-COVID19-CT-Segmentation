VANCOUVER (3 February) – Poalesco unveiled its annual Plant Showcase today. According to Poalesco spokesman Nacio Roja, this yearly offering highlights the company’s latest efforts in botanical research. Many of these efforts result from customer surveys designed to gain an understanding of common challenges. The research and development team then works to develop varietals that address these difficulties. This year, the company’s specialized nurseries have turned out drought-hardy breeds, such as the Goldtone Apple Tree. These varietals can withstand extended dry conditions without sustaining damage. “Gardeners in desert regions will appreciate the Goldtones in particular,” noted Roja. “And they might also be interested in our new Q7 rose bushes, which thrive in a similar climate.”
